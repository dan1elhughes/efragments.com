---
title: Grid Works
---

These designs were inspired by a colleague of mine, Ranko Bon, with whom I worked for many years. His passion for cave art and his imaginitive use of the simplest forms of expression eventually rubbed off on me. The boards he painted with this kind of design were 30x20 cms. I started to produce these designs on my computer using Microsoft Word to have a bit of fun at Ranko's expense. Before long, I was drawn into this strange pursuit and became fascinated by the creation of different forms within a very simple framework. I settled for pixel-editing these in MS Paint, then moved to a pixel editor app in Android. Very simple images at 84 x 60 pixels. The basic 6x4 grid can also be represented as 3x2 and other forms occur when the dominant marks are between grid points, rather than on them. Black and white is dominant, often with confusion about whether they are black on white, or white on blac. And the only colour used for accents or highlights is red, which should never touch black. Every element is a multiple of 3 pixels, except where the numbers don't add up.

# An encounter with a puppy

_11 June 2011_

I was in the Balkans, visiting people. I had been left in charge of my friend's apartment for a few hours, while he and his wife went shopping.  I was also looking after their puppy and another visitor, Anne, someone whom I knew, but only remotely, and I had not seen her for quite a few years. My friend's puppy seemed very soft and had long hair and floppy ears. She was quite tiny, about 25 cm long.  Black and tan and white, especially white on the underside. I cannot remember what her name was, but for some reason, I was holding her, and across the room from me was Anne. Anne was a large lady in her 40s, with too much make up and spikey hair, and over-designed spectacles. In fact, most things about Anne were overdone. But when she saw the puppy, she cooed and clucked and said come to me darling, come to me, over and over to the puppy. I knew that the puppy did not take kindly to strangers at all. But there was something very welcoming and unthreatening about Anne, so I kneeled on the polished wooden floor with the puppy, and whispered to her, go on, see what she has got for you. 

I encouraged the puppy, and to my surprise, she waddled uncertainly over to Anne, her soft claws clattering on the hard floor. From her voluminous bag, Anne had immediately produced a small collection of puppy-like toys and distractions. As she got closer to Anne, one of her paws slipped on the polished floor, and she pinned her ears back and went even more floppy, using all the submissive gestures in her armoury. Anne reached out with warm and syrupy noises and swept the little thing up. The innocuous scene was suddenly transformed for me, because I thought she was just wanting to cuddle and fuss the little thing, but I was shocked when I heard what she said as she kissed the puppy's face and belly, spreading its legs to make it even more submissive - that's it my little darling, she cooed, let's see if you are the reincarnation of my poor husband or children… I realized that she was the only survivor of an awful attack in the war that split Yugoslavia into the separate countries it now was. There was little visible evidence of the war, but clearly many very dreadful personal stories just beneath the surface. I found myself wondering how it could be that this woman was all pathos and no bitterness. But I suppose there must be some kind of psychological mechanisms in the brain to enable warring tribes to settle their differences and some to some kind of co-existence. 

All this from an encounter with a little puppy.
